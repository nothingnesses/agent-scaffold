# Q-64 explorer note: the checkable-versus-promptable partition

Lens: partition the CURRENT AGENTS.md content rule by rule into CHECKABLE (a machine can verify conformance or own the transition) versus PROMPTABLE-only (requires judgment, or is unobservable behavioral compliance). The test, from Q-64: a rule moves INTO the tool only if it is checkable; a promptable-only rule can be tool-DELIVERED as prose but never enforced. This note grounds that partition in the real file (root `AGENTS.md`, the byte-identical `.agents/AGENTS.reference.md`, the pack sources under `pack/`, and the role prompts under `pack/prompts/`), and in what `src/workflow.rs` (W3/W4/W5) and `src/next.rs` (the FSM) already enforce.

A note on method and on one recurring trap. "Checkable" here means the tool can verify a rule's conformance or own its transition from data it can read. It does NOT mean the rule's human purpose can be dropped from prose: a checkable rule can still keep a human-readable PROJECTION (Principle 8, Structured-data-first-project-for-humans), but that projection is GENERATED, not authored, so it cannot drift. The trap the lens must watch: a rule can look checkable while resting on an unverifiable input. The clearest case is the review-round OUTCOME. W3 checks the streak arithmetic over a sequence of `clean`/`new_valid` outcomes, but each outcome is the orchestrator's transcription of the triager's JUDGMENT that a finding was valid or not. The accounting is checkable; the truthfulness of the label it counts is promptable. Almost every "checkable" row below is really "checkable accounting over promptable inputs," and that is exactly why the floor cannot reach zero.

## 2. The concrete irreducible floor

Distilled from the real file, the floor is the minimal content that must stay AUTHORED prose at any tool maturity. It has three parts, and it is smaller than AGENTS.md today because most of the file is either checkable machinery or judgment prompts that already live in `pack/prompts/`.

Floor (a): the judgment-eliciting prompts. The authored source of `reviewer.md`'s "assume there are issues," `triager.md`'s "judge on evidence, not who raised it," `planner.md`'s recommendation duties, and the human-input contract's "options + trade-offs + recommendation + reasoning judged against the plan principles." The tool DELIVERS these (via `next.next_instruction.prompt_path` + `principle_reminders`) and can require the ARTIFACT they produce (a findings file, a `type:"decision"` receipt), but cannot verify their quality. These are already single files; the floor is that they stay authored, not that they stay in AGENTS.md.

Floor (b): the behavioral-compliance rules that only an artifact can proxy. Front-load context; reviewer independence and reading the diff; documentation currency; citing evidence. The tool can require the proxy artifact exists and is well-formed, never that the behavior happened. This is the permanent gap between "a round record exists" and "a real adversarial review occurred."

Floor (c): the self-bootstrap stub. The handful of loaded-by-default lines that must tell an agent, on a harness that does not auto-run the binary, that the tool exists and how to invoke it (the "canonical guidance ... point here rather than duplicate" header, the getting-started paragraph, the kickoff/resume pointers). This is irreducible because it is the one thing that cannot itself be delivered BY the tool the agent does not yet know to run.

What is NOT in the floor: everything in the CHECKABLE table. Its human-readable prose can remain, but as a GENERATED projection of the single workflow source (the direction Q-64's synthesis and Principle 8 point at), so those copies cannot drift. The Convergence section already demonstrates this ("generated from the workflow spec ... cannot drift"), as does the isolation policy ("settled here once and rendered from this single source"). The floor is the AUTHORED residue after those projections are subtracted: (a) + (b) as authored prompt/contract sources, plus (c) as a bootstrap stub.

## 3. Fraction estimate: movable versus permanent

By content weight of the current 56 KB AGENTS.md, roughly:

- ~40-50% is checkable machinery: the Convergence rule, Tracking progress, Preventing relitigation (ledger), the mechanical parts of Phases 3-5, and the entire Instrumentation schema. This is CHECKABLE and either already enforced (W3/W4/W5, `render --check`, `validate`) or generable as a projection of `workflow.toml` + the plan TOML. It is movable from authored source to generated projection; it does not need to stay authored prose.
- ~40-50% is promptable judgment/behavioral prose: the role descriptions, the behavioral content of the phases, the human-input contract's judgment core, the Socratic/exploration/review entry modes, prose formatting, and the Principles list. This is PROMPTABLE. It can be tool-DELIVERED (and much of it already is, via the role prompts `next` points at), but its quality is never enforced. Most of it duplicates `pack/prompts/*.md`, so the AGENTS.md copy is a delivery redundancy, not an authored necessity.
- ~5-10% is bootstrap and human-contract framing: floor (c) plus the human-facing "why."

So the movable-versus-permanent split is NOT 50/50 on the file. The checkable ~45% can leave AGENTS.md as authored prose (become generated projection); most of the promptable ~45% can also leave (it is delivered from `pack/prompts/` by `next`). The genuinely irreducible AUTHORED floor is small: the ~5-10% bootstrap stub, plus the authored SOURCE of the judgment prompts and the human-input contract (which live in `pack/`, not AGENTS.md, already). A matured AGENTS.md could shrink to a generated stub plus the bootstrap contract, with everything else projected.

Load-bearing examples that prove the movement is real, not aspirational:
- CHECKABLE, already moved: the Convergence "control constants" sentence is explicitly a projection of `workflow.toml` and says so.
- CHECKABLE, already moved: "Who isolates is settled here once and rendered from this single source" is `ISOLATION_POLICY_FRAGMENT`, emitted both into the AGENTS.md slot and by `next`.
- CHECKABLE, planned boundary: "the deterministic `validate --workflow` check, once built, is the backstop that the required reviewed rounds happened before a step is marked complete." W3 (the detector) is built; the `--strict` blocking gate is not. This is the single sharpest already-named checkable-but-unbuilt item.
- PROMPTABLE, permanent: `reviewer.md`'s "make it your goal to find where it is wrong." No arithmetic reaches the quality of that.
- The trap, load-bearing: W3 counts `clean`/`new_valid` outcomes but each is a transcription of the triager's judgment. Convergence is checkable ACCOUNTING over a promptable INPUT. This is why enforcement can guarantee the ceremony ran (rounds happened, receipts exist, the view matches its source) but never that the judgment inside the ceremony was sound. That boundary is Q-64's floor, made mechanical.

Judged against the plan's 8 principles by name: Make-illegal-states-unrepresentable (P5) is why the checkable pile is real and growing (typed `StepStatus`, the closed FSM state set, W3/W4/W5 turning "marked complete without review" into a reported violation). Structured-data-first-project-for-humans (P8) is why the checkable prose should become a generated projection rather than authored duplicate, and why the promptable floor's human-readable framing stays as generated output. Ground-decisions-in-evidence (P6) cautions that the planned rungs (`record-*`, `validate --workflow --strict`) are worth building only where drift or a real missed-review has been observed, not speculatively; W3/W4/W5 earned their place by catching real drift (the driver principle-numbering bug, the W4-baseline doc drift named in Q-64). Minimal-by-default (P2) says the floor should stay a stub, not grow: the judgment prompts and bootstrap are the core the tool cannot cross, and piling deliverable-but-unenforceable prose back into AGENTS.md would violate it.

## Identity read (for Q-65)

The checkable/promptable balance says the project is now primarily a structured WORKFLOW ENGINE / DRIVER, not a scaffolding tool. The evidence: three enforced structural invariants (W3/W4/W5) that own convergence accounting, waiver integrity, and decision-receipt presence; a closed-state FSM (`next`) that reconstructs loop state and emits the just-in-time instruction; `render`/`validate` that pin the human view to a structured source; and a single control-constant spec (`workflow.toml`) the arithmetic reads. "Scaffold" now names only the one-shot `scaffold` subcommand (the initial file drop), which is the least of what the binary does; the centre of gravity is validating, projecting, and driving an ongoing workflow. The checkable pile is precisely the engine's surface, and it is real and enforced, not aspirational, so the engine identity is earned rather than claimed.

Implication for a rename (Q-65): the public name should project the engine/driver identity (a workflow driver/conductor), because P8 and P1 argue the name should accurately reflect what the tool IS, and "scaffold" mis-projects it as a one-shot generator. The one caution is P2 (Minimal-by-default): scaffolding is still the entry point a new project meets first, so the rename should elevate the workflow-engine identity without implying the scaffolder is gone; the scaffolder becomes one subcommand of a driver, not the whole tool. The irreducible floor supports this framing rather than undercutting it: the floor is small and stable (bootstrap stub + judgment prompts), so the tool's growing weight sits in the enforced engine, which is what the name should describe.
