### `escalation-exempt`: W3 accepts a human-authorized below-streak convergence (`Q-40`)

Deferred, folded into `Q-44`. This step would add a THIRD W3-exemption special-case (`escalation` acceptance) alongside `grandfathered` and `trivial`. The human's 2026-07-18 architecture pivot (Project Principle 8) and cleanup mandate identified exemption proliferation as a sharp edge to UNIFY rather than extend, so do NOT build this as a one-off; its need (representing a human-authorised below-streak convergence so W3 does not false-flag it) is carried into `Q-44`'s exemption-unification design, and the approach recorded below is the input to that, not a standalone build. Original coupling note (still true): it reads the existing `type:"escalation"` record, which the receipt-encoding passes confirmed is independent of the decision-receipt mechanism. Decided (`Q-40`, human, 2026-07-18): the ESCALATION-RECORD-AWARE approach. Problem: `optional-modules` increment 2c-ii was `risky` (W3 requires peak `consecutive_clean` >= 2) but the human accepted it at ONE clean round via the escalation contract at the 5-round total-cap (the write-escape class was closed and independently confirmed). Its round records therefore show risky/peak-clean-1, which W3 will false-flag as non-converged once `optional-modules` is marked `complete`. Neither `trivial` (review was not skipped) nor `grandfathered` (it is not pre-logging) fits.

The decided fix: W3, when an increment's peak `consecutive_clean` falls short of its `risk_class` requirement, does NOT flag it if that increment's records are accompanied by a `type:"escalation"` record with `human_decision:"decision"` (matched by the increment's `task`); that record is the durable evidence the human authorized convergence, so W3 treats the increment as converged. Chosen over a new Roadmap override status/marker (new vocabulary + a drift surface where the marker can disagree with the log) and over a new accepted-risk annotation field (new schema for what the escalation record already implies), because it reuses data ALREADY in the log (the `optional-modules-inc2cii` escalation record is present, record 83), adds no new schema or status, and ties the exemption precisely to the decision that authorized it (auditable). Principle-grounded: Principle 16 (one source of truth, the escalation record is the single authority for the decision) and Principle 2 (minimal, no new machinery).

Build spec: in `src/workflow.rs`, the per-increment convergence check gains an escalation-exemption branch. Precise semantics: (a) the exemption fires ONLY on `human_decision:"decision"`, never `"resume"` (a resume is not an acceptance); (b) it is scoped to the one increment whose `task` matches the escalation record, never a blanket bypass; (c) it exempts ONLY the clean-streak shortfall, the `risk_class`-consistency check and every other W3 check still apply; (d) a `complete` step whose increment converged this way passes W3 with an informational note rather than an error. Tests: an increment with risky/peak-clean-1 records PLUS an escalation-decision record passes; the same records WITHOUT the escalation record still flag; the same records with a `"resume"` escalation still flag; risk_class inconsistency still flags even with an escalation record. Dogfood acceptance: after this lands, marking `optional-modules` `complete` and running `validate --workflow` on this repo's own plan exits 0. Risk note for review: this makes W3 trust the escalation record as authority; that is intended (the record IS the human decision), but reviewers should probe that the exemption cannot be widened accidentally (scoping (a)-(c)). Classified RISKY at loop-open (it edits the enforcement backstop that guards workflow integrity, the same load-bearing surface `workflow-invariants` built) -> two consecutive clean rounds.
