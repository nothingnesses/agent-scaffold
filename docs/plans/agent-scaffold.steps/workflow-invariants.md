The key invariant (W3): for every Roadmap step marked `complete`, filter `round` records whose `task` leading-slug (before an optional `-inc<x>` suffix) equals the step slug, then GROUP those records BY INCREMENT (the leading slug plus its optional `-inc<x>` suffix); within each increment the `risk_class` must be consistent and the consecutive-clean streak (the peak over that increment's records, since `consecutive_clean` is a per-loop running streak that spans rounds naming different artifacts) must reach the required count for that class (`low_risk` 1, `risky` 2). W3 checks ONLY `complete` steps. A `complete` step with no matching round records is the `pause.md` catch, UNLESS it carries one of the two review-exempt statuses below.

The two owed sub-decisions are now settled (both decided by the human in the design pass):

- (A) A declared trivial completion uses a new `trivial` Roadmap status in `plan.rs` (added to `ROADMAP_STATUSES`, so the plan-template drift guard covers it): a `trivial` step declares a review-skipped completion, single-sourced in the status column, drift-guard-covered, and the hardest of the candidates to fake (chosen over a log record for a round that never happened). W3 does not require rounds for a `trivial` step, so a silent skip becomes a visible, declared one.
- (B) A `grandfathered` Roadmap status (a parallel terminal status in `plan.rs`) exempts pre-logging legacy steps from W3. Chosen over a positional cutoff (brittle to future insertion, repo-specific) and a closed slug list (a separate artifact that drifts): a status is coherent with the `trivial` choice, insertion-safe, and general for any project. The earliest steps that predate logging are relabelled `grandfathered` when this lands.
